Why Playful Movement Works

Regular physical activity improves thinking skills and lowers the risk of cognitive decline. Learning new, coordinated skills builds brain connectivity. Exercise also supports people with Parkinson’s — improving gait, balance, and quality of life.

What the Research Says

Studies on older adults show that regular physical activity improves cognitive function and brain structure. One study found that open‑skill activities (for example, tennis or basketball) are associated with better inhibition, visual tracking and cognitive flexibility, while closed‑skill activities (such as swimming or yoga) are linked to better selective attention and visuospatial function【240476014268509†L100-L116】. This means that challenging yourself with playful movement at any age can enhance various mental skills.

Regular physical activity is also correlated with increases in white‑matter and parietal‑lobe gray‑matter volume, hippocampal and basal‑ganglia volume, and upregulation of brain‑derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF)【240476014268509†L124-L141】. BDNF supports neuronal growth, synaptic development and neuroplasticity, which helps protect against age‑related cognitive decline.

Health & Wellness Keynote Benefits for Organizations

Inviting a health and wellness keynote speaker can inspire teams and create a positive culture of movement and mindfulness. In addition to teaching practical exercises, these sessions can:

  • Improve productivity: Active employees tend to be more focused and energetic, which can translate into higher productivity and better problem‑solving.
  • Reduce absenteeism: Regular movement and stress‑management practices help decrease sick days and prevent injury.
  • Boost morale: Shared experiences around play and wellness build camaraderie and foster a more positive, supportive workplace.
  • Enhance creativity: Engaging the brain through novel physical challenges encourages flexible thinking and innovation.

Cognition

  • Moderate‑to‑vigorous activity is linked with better cognitive performance and reduced dementia risk.
  • Skill learning (e.g., hand–eye drills) increases brain connectivity via neuroplasticity.

Parkinson’s & Neurological Support

  • Exercise upregulates neurotrophic factors and supports better motor and non‑motor outcomes.
  • Balance and coordination drills can complement medical care (consult your clinician first).
