Science‑Backed Benefits of Play

Research shows that playful movement is powerful for your brain and body. Here’s what scientists have discovered:

Brain & Cognitive Health

  • Moderate‑to‑vigorous physical activity lowers the risk of cognitive decline and dementia and improves cognitive function at all ages.
  • Learning new motor skills like juggling strengthens brain connections and increases white‑matter connectivity, showing that the brain can rewire itself at any age.
  • Regular movement and hand–eye coordination challenges stimulate neuroplasticity and may slow the progression of neurodegenerative diseases.

Body & Well‑Being

  • Playful exercise improves balance, coordination and dexterity while boosting cardiovascular health.
  • Physical activity releases “feel‑good” hormones that reduce anxiety and depression and improve mood and sleep quality.
  • Studies show that 2.5 hours of activity per week can enhance mobility and complement traditional therapy for conditions like Parkinson’s disease.
